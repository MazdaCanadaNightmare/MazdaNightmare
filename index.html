Mazda Canada Nightmare: Our Family’s 7-Month Ordeal

What Mazda and Century Mazda put us through for a safety issue they admitted—and then ignored.

We paid full price—cash—for a brand new CX-90. For over 7 months, we’ve been misled, dismissed, and left driving a vehicle with known safety issues. What started with a headlight TSB became a nightmare of delays, dishonesty, and finger-pointing by both Mazda Canada and Century Mazda in Halifax.

Official Mazda TSB: Flickering Front Light When Raining

Mazda issued a Technical Service Bulletin (TSB No. 09-037/24, dated August 1, 2024) for water intrusion into the front headlamp. The dealership initially dismissed it, saying it “wasn’t raining hard enough” to see the issue.

🚨 Detailed Safety Timeline

December 2024

We report visibility problems due to headlamp flickering. The dealership dismisses the issue, claiming the rain “wasn’t heavy enough.” No inspection is done. We offer to pay out of pocket to install the TSB fix. They agree—but don’t follow through.

January 2025

We return and show the TSB directly to Josh at Century Mazda. He agrees to order the part. Later, Mazda Canada tells us the part was “installed” before Mazda’s own records say it was ordered—clear miscommunication or false record-keeping.

March 2025

We follow up and discover the part still wasn’t ordered. Only after persistent outreach does the dealership admit it and finally place the order—three months after they said they would.

April 2025

While the vehicle is in for service, it is hit by another car—on their lot. Initially, Century Mazda blames another customer. We later find out it was their own lot attendant, with a history of damaging vehicles. Only when we threatened legal action were we shown video footage confirming their lie.

May 2025

We raise concerns about dangerous acceleration lag when merging. Mazda’s solution? “Floor it, let up, then floor it again.” This is not a real fix—it’s a liability, especially with children in the car.

June 2025

Still no resolution. Multiple emails and service messages are ignored. No diagnostics booked. Mazda EV techs involved, but nothing changes. We are told a service manager will follow up. They never do.

July 30, 2025

We formally request our case be escalated to Mazda Canada leadership: Amy Flemming (incoming CEO), David Klan (CEO), and Makota Yoshihara (Japan Compliance). Instead, we are told we can "trade in the vehicle" and let another family inherit the risk.

July 31, 2025

We call Mazda Canada again. Haley confirms Jason received our email. We ask for confirmation that leadership saw it. No follow-up is provided. Our family continues driving an unsafe vehicle.

August 1, 2025

We call again and speak with Jenny. She admits Jason saw the email but says he’s "waiting on more dealership notes" before responding. No response ever comes. No one calls us back.
